Ryan Shea

bio

Hi, my name is Ryan Shea. I study computer science and mechanical engineering at Princeton University and am Co-President of the Princeton Entrepreneurship Club. I am an aspiring entrepreneur and have a passion for building things - from web and mobile apps to the entrepreneurial community at Princeton.

In the past I’ve interned as a Software Engineer at ZocDoc, co-founded a startup with one of the creators of turntable, co-organized the first Startup Weekend at Princeton (which has since inspired a series of Princeton hackathons), led Princeton’s annual startup challenge (TigerLaunch) through its biggest year to date, served as curator for one of the first StartupDigest university issues, co-organized the first “hack week” at Princeton (with over 75 students building apps over winter break), and introduced three student-run programming classes (with a waitlist in the hundreds) to the Princeton campus.

Nowadays, I spend a lot of my time building web and mobile apps. Python has long been my language of choice, but recently its reign as my favorite has been challenged by Objective-C. Since beginning my exploration into the world of mobile apps, I have become particularly enamored by iOS development. I plan on publishing my first app to the App Store sometime this March or April.

After graduating from Princeton in May, it is my goal to work at a startup in San Francisco or New York City. I plan to acquire more experience by joining a seasoned team that is deeply passionate about building high quality mobile products. Finally, it is my goal to one day take the plunge and start my own company - a dream I have had since a very young age!
