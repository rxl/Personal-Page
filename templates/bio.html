Ryan Shea

What I've Done

Over the past few years I’ve interned as a Software Engineer at ZocDoc, co-founded a startup with one of the creators of turntable, built up the Princeton Entrepreneurship Club to become one of the most influential student organizations on campus (with a few dozen officers, over 200 active members and 1,200 email subscribers), co-organized the first Startup Weekend at Princeton (which has since inspired a series of Princeton hackathons), led Princeton’s annual startup challenge (TigerLaunch) through its biggest year to date, served as curator for one of the first StartupDigest university issues, co-organized the first “hack week” at Princeton (with over 75 students building apps over winter break), and introduced three student-run programming classes (with a waitlist in the hundreds) to the Princeton campus.

What I'm Doing Now

Nowadays, I spend a lot of my time building web and mobile apps. Python has long been my language of choice, but recently its reign as my favorite has been challenged by Objective-C. Since beginning my exploration into the world of mobile apps, I have become particularly enamored by iOS development. I plan on publishing my first app to the App Store sometime this March or April.

What I Hope to Do Next

After graduating from Princeton in May, it is my goal to work at a startup in San Francisco or New York City. I plan to acquire more experience by joining a seasoned team that is deeply passionate about building high quality mobile products. Finally, it is my goal to one day take the plunge and start my own company - a dream I have had since a very young age!
